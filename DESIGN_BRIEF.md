# Design Brief — Karmell

## Project Overview

**Brand:** Karmell  
**Category:** Bespoke cakes, pastries and small-batch sweet bakes made to order  
**Founder:** Karin  
**Format:** Single-page editorial landing page  
**Primary goal:** Create immediate trust, desire and emotional resonance, then guide the visitor naturally toward inquiry or order  
**Business reality:** This is not a bakery chain, not e-commerce, not a discount-driven shop. It is a personal craft brand built on care, taste, detail and feeling.

**Core challenge:**  
The page must feel premium without becoming cold, and intimate without becoming rustic or amateur.  
It should feel like a carefully wrapped gift, not like a sales funnel.

**The one sentence to remember:**  
Karmell is a quiet luxury craft brand for meaningful moments.

---

## Creative Direction

### Central idea
**Quiet celebration.**

The website should feel like the digital equivalent of opening a beautifully wrapped handmade gift before an important gathering.  
There is softness, restraint, anticipation and tactility.  
The atmosphere should invite the visitor in slowly, with confidence, warmth and composure.

### Emotional arc
The page should not feel like a sequence of content blocks.  
It should unfold like a short editorial story:

1. Wonder  
2. Trust  
3. Desire  
4. Reassurance  
5. Invitation

### Brand personality
- Warm, but never sugary
- Premium, but never distant
- Feminine, but not overly romantic
- Minimal, but never empty
- Crafted, not corporate
- Personal, not casual

### Positioning
If most cake websites feel transactional, festive or decorative, Karmell must feel curated, composed and deeply considered.

---

## What Makes This Award-Worthy

This project should aim beyond “clean and beautiful”.  
It should feel art-directed.

That means:
- A strong visual thesis, not generic premium styling
- Editorial pacing, not repetitive section stacking
- Intentional asymmetry, not template symmetry
- Selective motion, not animation everywhere
- Material warmth, not flat UI surfaces
- Photography-led atmosphere, not image dumping
- Distinctive typography rhythm, not safe SaaS spacing
- Mobile experience that feels designed, not merely reduced

If a design decision feels common, expected or interchangeable, it is probably not strong enough.

---

## What To Avoid

- Generic “luxury brand” clichés without personality
- Overused startup minimalism
- Purple or blue-tinted gradients
- Cold white backgrounds
- Hard borders and boxy UI
- Constant reveal animations on everything
- Obvious template rhythms
- Cheap bakery tropes
- Cute decorative icons that undermine premium feel
- Loud, sales-heavy CTA language
- Excessive text that explains instead of evokes
- “AI-generated aesthetic” energy

---

## Experience Principles

### 1. The page must feel composed, not assembled
Each section should feel like part of one visual narrative.  
Avoid the feeling of independent blocks stacked vertically.

### 2. White space is not emptiness
Negative space is part of the luxury language.  
Breathing room should create confidence and visual poise.

### 3. Restraint creates elegance
Do fewer things, but do them more intentionally.  
If everything is emphasized, nothing feels special.

### 4. Imperfect balance feels human
Allow moments of asymmetry, offset alignment and editorial tension.  
Do not over-regularize the layout.

### 5. Sensory detail beats marketing language
Texture, crop, light, material and copy nuance should do more work than promotional claims.

## Art Direction References

Use references only as directional mood, never as templates to imitate.

**Relevant worlds:**
- editorial food photography
- luxury skincare storytelling
- boutique hospitality websites
- independent magazine layout systems
- refined packaging design
- printed lookbooks

**Do not directly mimic:**
Aesop clones, Glossier clones, generic beige DTC layouts, Pinterest “luxury bakery” moodboards.

The goal is not to look familiar.  
The goal is to look inevitable and memorable.

## Color Palette

```css
:root {
  --cream: #FDF6EE;
  --caramel: #C8854A;
  --blush: #E8C4B0;
  --espresso: #3D2314;
  --latte: #9C7B6B;
  --white: #FFFFFF;
  --mocha: #6E4A36;
  --linen: #F6EEE5;
  --golden-shadow: rgba(200, 133, 74, 0.18);
  --warm-shadow: rgba(61, 35, 20, 0.08);
}
```

### Usage rules
- Base page background is always `--cream` or a warmer tonal variation, never pure white
- `--white` is reserved for isolated surfaces, image framing or moments of contrast
- `--caramel` is an accent, not a dominant color
- `--espresso` is the anchor for typography and contrast
- `--blush` and `--linen` should create tonal layering, not candy-like sweetness
- Shadows must be warm and diffused, never grey and muddy

### Color behavior
The page should feel tonal, not colorful.  
Think in layers of warmth rather than in separate blocks of color.

---

## Materiality

A key part of the premium feel is **surface quality**.

### Material direction
- backgrounds may use extremely subtle paper-like grain or soft radial warmth
- cards and panels should feel like layered material, not flat rectangles
- image framing should feel curated and tactile
- shadow should suggest atmosphere, not UI elevation

### Surface rules
- no harsh inner shadows
- no glossy glassmorphism as a main style
- no busy textures
- any texture must be nearly imperceptible
- depth should come from tonal separation, crop, spacing and light

---

## Typography

### Font pairing
- **Display:** elegant serif with character and contrast  
  Preferred options: `Cormorant Garamond`, `Canela-style`, `DM Serif Display`, `Bodoni Moda`
- **Body/UI:** refined sans with warmth and clarity  
  Preferred options: `Plus Jakarta Sans`, `Manrope`, `Sora`, `Outfit`, `DM Sans`

Avoid `Inter`, `Roboto`, default system-safe blandness, or pairings that feel too tech/startup.

### Typographic behavior
Typography should create the page rhythm almost by itself.  
The serif should carry emotion and prestige.  
The sans should bring clarity and restraint.

### Hierarchy
- Hero display: `clamp(4.5rem, 8vw, 7rem)`
- Section display: `clamp(2.5rem, 5vw, 4.25rem)`
- Body: `17px–19px`
- Small UI text: `14px–15px`
- Labels / metadata: `12px–13px`, uppercase, tracked

### Rules
- Use generous line-height in body copy
- Tighten display letter-spacing slightly
- Allow some lines to wrap beautifully rather than forcing perfect symmetry
- Headings should feel shaped, not merely typed
- Avoid long text blocks wider than comfortable reading measure
- Let large type create contrast and pause

---

## Copy Direction

The copy must sound like a human craft brand with taste, not like a service page.

### Copy principles
- Be sensory, not salesy
- Be specific, not generic
- Be calm, not pushy
- Be warm, not overly sentimental
- Say less, but mean more

### Avoid phrases like
- “quality service”
- “made with love”
- “for every occasion”
- “we offer”
- “best cakes”
- “customer satisfaction”

Unless such claims are grounded in detail, they weaken the brand.

### Preferred tone
- understated
- evocative
- precise
- intimate
- elegant

### Headline guidance
Section titles should not read like admin labels.  
Prefer emotionally resonant phrasing over utility naming.

For example:
- Not: `Mida pakume`
- Better: `Magusad vormid erilisteks hetkedeks`

- Not: `Kuidas tellimine käib`
- Better: `Tellimine, rahulikult ja selgelt`

---

## Layout & Composition

### Global rules
- Content max width: `1280px`
- Sections should not all start and end with the same rhythm
- Use wrapper discipline for content, but allow occasional visual breakout moments
- At least 2 sections should contain controlled asymmetry
- At least 2 moments on desktop should feel editorial rather than grid-perfect

### Editorial composition principles
- One section may be text-heavy and image-light
- The next may invert that rhythm
- A quote, image or heading may intentionally overscale
- Some visual elements may slightly trespass beyond the expected content grid
- The experience should feel paced, not uniform

### Responsive philosophy
Mobile is not a collapsed desktop.  
It must preserve elegance, hierarchy and atmosphere on a 390px screen.

On mobile:
- spacing must still feel luxurious
- typography must still feel expressive
- image crops must remain intentional
- sticky and scrollytelling behavior must degrade gracefully if needed

## Photography Direction

This is one of the highest-leverage areas of the entire project.

### Photography must communicate
- texture
- care
- ingredient quality
- stillness
- occasion
- human touch

### Preferred image language
- soft natural side-light
- creamy highlights
- restrained contrast
- tactile closeups
- intentional crop
- negative space around subject
- subtle background styling

### Avoid
- harsh flash
- overly saturated images
- generic top-down e-commerce shots
- cluttered kitchen scenes
- busy props
- inconsistent color temperature
- random image aspect ratios without curation

### Required image mix
The gallery and hero should together create a narrative mix:
- one iconic hero cake image
- one macro texture/detail frame
- one process/hands frame
- one celebratory atmosphere frame
- one product grouping or pastry composition
- one emotional lifestyle frame

### Image handling
- cropping is part of design
- not all images need equal prominence
- fewer stronger images beat many average ones
- if source photography is weak, the layout must compensate through crop, whitespace and pacing, not decoration

## Motion Philosophy

Motion is essential, but it must feel editorial and expensive, never busy.

### Motion principles
- Movement should feel like breath, not effect
- Use motion to reveal hierarchy, not to decorate every object
- Parallax must be restrained and smooth
- Use stagger only where it adds rhythm
- Never animate everything in a section by default
- Motion should create confidence, not call attention to itself

### Mandatory quality bar
- smooth at 60fps
- no jank on scroll
- reduced motion supported
- no autoplay gimmicks
- no constant floating, pulsing or looping distractions

### Use motion mainly for
- hero entrance
- section transitions
- scrollytelling state changes
- subtle image emphasis
- CTA focus and hover depth
- testimonial or gallery interaction if user-triggered

### Important correction
Do **not** attach reveal animation to every heading, paragraph, image and card on the page.
That pattern is too common and cheapens the experience.
Use it selectively and with hierarchy.

### Kihilisus ja parallax
Kasutatakse parallax-efekte, kus tekst ja pildid liiguvad erineva kiirusega, tekitades sügavusmõõtme.

- Iga parallax-kiht liigub erineva teguriga (nt 0.1× / 0.25× / 0.4×)
- Vähemalt hero ja üks sisulik sektsioon kasutavad kihilist parallax-liikumist
- Eesmärk: atmosfäär ja ruumilisus, mitte silmatorkaval viisil liikuv efekt
- Alati `requestAnimationFrame` + `passive: true` — mitte kunagi scroll-jank

---

## Navigation

### Intent
The navigation should feel like it belongs to a premium editorial object, not an app shell.

### Behavior
- transparent or near-invisible on load
- transforms into warm frosted surface on scroll
- subtle blur, subtle shadow, no heavy line separators
- always elegant and quiet

### Structure
- logo/wordmark left
- a small number of navigation links right
- one clear CTA
- spacing must feel balanced and breathable

### Mobile nav
- avoid generic heavy hamburger drawer styling
- if collapsed, it should still feel refined, warm and lightweight

---

## Buttons & Interactive Elements

### Principle
Buttons should feel tactile, calm and premium.

### Primary CTA
- warm caramel fill
- soft pill radius
- generous horizontal padding
- subtle lift on hover
- no aggressive bounce

### Secondary CTA
- quieter, more editorial
- can be outlined or text-link style if it supports the layout better
- should not compete equally with the primary

### Hover behavior
- slight scale or rise only
- shadow warmth should increase gently
- transitions should feel soft and precise
- focus states must remain visible and accessible

### Avoid
- loud gradients
- chunky borders
- overly playful hover transforms
- hyperactive microinteractions

---

## Cards & Panels

Cards should not feel like dashboard components.  
They should feel like curated content surfaces.

### Rules
- rounded corners can be used, but not excessively
- shadow should be feathered and warm
- border usage should be minimal to none
- internal spacing should be generous
- cards may differ slightly in size or emphasis when helpful

## Required Sections

## 1. Hero

### Purpose
Immediate emotion and trust.  
The visitor should feel the brand before they evaluate the offer.

### Content
- wordmark or elegant brand name
- minimal navigation
- emotionally resonant headline
- short supporting copy
- primary CTA
- secondary CTA
- one hero image with authority and air

### Direction
This section must be cinematic in restraint.  
Do not overcrowd it.  
Large type, soft contrast, one image, real breathing room.

### Recommended headline territory
Use lines with atmosphere, not category-first explanation.

Good direction:
- `Magusad hetked, tehtud südamega`
- `Tordid ja maiused, mis jäävad meelde`
- `Käsitööna loodud maitsed erilisteks sündmusteks`

### Hero quality bar
If the hero is generic, the whole site will feel generic.

---

## 2. Signature Offering Section

Rename from `Mida pakume` to something more elevated.

Suggested titles:
- `Magusad vormid erilisteks hetkedeks`
- `Valmistatud sinu sündmuse rütmis`
- `Kolm viisi, kuidas hetk magusamaks muutub`

### Purpose
Introduce the offer categories through a stronger narrative device than a normal service grid.

### Structure
Pinned or semi-pinned storytelling section with category transitions.

### Categories
- Tordid
- Koogid ja maiused
- Erisoovid

### Direction
The image should feel stable and desirable.  
The text transitions should feel composed and clear.  
This is one of the “signature experience” moments of the page.

### Important
If the scrollytelling feels technically heavy, simplify the engineering while preserving the feeling.  
Experience quality matters more than implementation purity.

## 3. Karmelli Approach

Suggested section titles:
- `Iga tellimus valmib hoole ja tundega`
- `Maitse, detail ja rahulik hool`
- `Käsitöö, mis on tunda igas kihis`

### Purpose
Create trust through philosophy, not sales claims.

### Content
- a calm portrait of the approach
- short founder-centered copy
- 3 proof points or values
- image of Karin, hands at work, or a restrained process moment

### Proof point direction
Avoid generic icon-list energy.  
Use either:
- elegant small symbols
- numbered principles
- or minimal text markers

Preferred proof points:
- Käsitööna
- Värskelt valmistatud
- Personaalse lähenemisega

---

## 4. Gallery

### Purpose
The strongest argument for quality and desire.

### Direction
This must feel curated like an editorial portfolio, not a product dump.

### Layout
- masonry or asymmetrical composition
- varied scale
- one or two larger anchor images
- room for crops that feel intentional
- slight overspill beyond the normal rhythm is allowed on large screens

### Interaction
- subtle hover scale or focus shift
- no flashy overlays unless truly elegant
- no noisy lightbox behavior by default

### Important
A weaker but beautifully art-directed gallery will outperform a larger but more average one.

---

## 5. Testimonials

### Purpose
Ground the visual beauty in social trust.

### Tone
Quiet, refined, generous spacing.  
These should feel like real voices in a beautiful printed spread.

### Direction
- oversized quotation mark used sparingly
- calm card treatment
- no auto-rotating carousel
- if slider is used, it must be user-controlled only

### Content
Use 3 to 5 short, believable testimonials.

### Design note
Testimonials should balance the image-heavy sections and provide emotional reassurance without breaking the mood.

---

## 6. Ordering Process

Rename from `Kuidas tellimine käib` to something more brand-aligned.

Suggested titles:
- `Tellimine, rahulikult ja selgelt`
- `Kõik algab ühest sõnumist`
- `Lihtne tee sinu soovini`

### Purpose
Remove friction and uncertainty.

### Structure
Three-step process with warmth and clarity.

### Steps
1. Kirjuta või helista  
2. Lepime detailid kokku  
3. Valmib midagi tõeliselt erilist

### Direction
This section can be slightly more structured than the others, but still elegant.  
Think refined guidance, not infographic.

---

## 7. Closing CTA

### Purpose
A final emotional invitation, not a pushy conversion block.

### Direction
This section should feel like a tonal shift with more emotional gravity.  
Darker background is welcome if it feels rich and warm.

### Suggested content
- headline with emotional pull
- one-line invitation
- one strong CTA

### Suggested headline territory
- `Sul on mõni tähtpäev tulemas?`
- `Loome koos midagi tõeliselt ilusat ja maitsvat`
- `Kui hetk on oluline, võiks ka maius seda olla`

### Quality bar
The CTA must feel like a beautiful close, not a sales wall.

### Purpose
Deliver practical information in a way that still feels on-brand.

### Content
- Karmell wordmark
- short brand line

### Direction
Warm, minimal, polished.

## Section Rhythm

The page should alternate between these modes:
- emotional image-led moment
- explanatory trust moment
- visual portfolio moment
- structured reassurance moment

Avoid repeating the same section logic more than twice in a row.

---

## Technical Direction

### Non-negotiables
- responsive on `390px`, `768px`, `1440px`
- smooth and performant
- accessible color contrast
- visible keyboard focus states
- reduced-motion support
- no unnecessary dependencies
- no design decisions that collapse under real content

### Wrapper rule
All content must remain inside a shared wrapper unless a deliberate visual breakout is defined.

```css
.wrapper {
  max-width: 1280px;
  margin: 0 auto;
  padding: 0 clamp(1.5rem, 5vw, 5rem);
}
```

Backgrounds may extend full width.  
Content should remain controlled.

### Implementation preference
- HTML + CSS + lightweight JS
- motion via CSS and efficient JS only where needed
- `IntersectionObserver` for reveal logic when used
- scroll effects only if they remain smooth and subtle
- no framework-heavy behavior unless explicitly needed by the project

### Important engineering note
Do not blindly copy animation code just because it was proposed earlier.  
The implementation may adapt if the final visual result is smoother, more elegant and more maintainable.

---

## Accessibility & Quality

A premium site that ignores accessibility is not premium.

### Required
- sufficient text contrast
- focus-visible states
- semantic HTML
- form labels and usability
- reduced motion support
- touch-friendly hit areas
- no essential information hidden behind hover only

### Performance
- optimize images properly
- avoid scroll jank
- avoid oversized scripts
- motion must not compromise clarity or responsiveness

---

## Build Priorities

Build in this order:

1. Hero mood, spacing and typography
2. Navigation behavior
3. Signature offering / scrollytelling section
4. Karmelli approach section
5. Gallery composition
6. Testimonials
7. Ordering section
8. Closing CTA
9. Footer and contact
10. Final polish pass for motion, mobile, hierarchy, crop and contrast

---

## Final Standard

Before considering the page finished, check:

- Does it feel like one curated story?
- Is the hero genuinely memorable?
- Is the motion restrained enough to feel expensive?
- Do the images feel art-directed rather than placed?
- Does the typography create rhythm on its own?
- Does the mobile version still feel luxurious?
- Does the page feel personal without becoming homemade?
- Does anything look interchangeable with a template?

If yes, keep refining.

---

## Final Reminder

Karmell is not selling cake in the generic sense.  
Karmell is offering care, beauty and taste for moments that matter.

The website should feel like that promise before the first inquiry is ever sent.
